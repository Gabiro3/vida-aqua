VIDAQUA RWANDA

VIDAQUA Rwanda, aims to educate and empower the Rilima community, address issues of water scarcity, pollution, and sustainable practices in the Rilima community, particularly in relation to Lake Kidogo. The lack of awareness about water conservation among residents near the lake exacerbates these challenges, threatening the future supply of clean water.

About Us

VIDAQUA Rwanda educates and empowers the Rilima community near Lake Kidogo on sustainable water conservation practices, addressing water scarcity, pollution, and the critical role of protecting the lake as a community resource.

  • Protecting Lake Kidogo

    Water Conservation Training for Fishermen and Farmers

    Together with the Urugerero team, VIDAQUA educates Rilima residents, particularly fishermen and farmers, on preventing water contamination. Key teachings include avoiding washing fishing nets in the lake, maintaining a ten-meter distance from the water, and not disposing of waste in the lake. VIDAQUA collaborates with the Rilima sector to plan training sessions, and so far, they have taught 50 fishermen, 40 farmers, and 34 traders near Lake Kidogo.
